Quantum Deep Graph Infomax

2 November 2025·158 words·1 min

We propose an end-to-end quantum graph convolutional network (QGCN) encoder that is fully compatible with noisy intermediate-scale quantum (NISQ) devices. Our design relies entirely on native one-qubit or two-qubit gates, which makes it hardware-efficient and compiler-friendly compared to other quantum neural network encoders. The core of our approach is the combination of a NISQ-compatible encoder with Deep Graph Infomax (DGI), enabling unsupervised learning tasks in a complete quantum pipeline.

We validate our model on two representative datasets. For the Cora citation graph, which has been widely used in both supervised and unsupervised graph learning benchmarks, where our method achieves competitive clustering performance while maintaining reduced circuit depth and gate counts. More importantly, we explore the SNP genomic dataset, introduced through the PennyLane library, where we construct graphs from genomic haplotype data. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first attempt to apply end-to-end quantum GCN as encoder of DGI for unsupervised learning to genomic graph construction.
